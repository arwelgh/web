Description

A profound exploration of the influential Aquino family's legacy in the Philippines, penned by Nick Joaquin. This essay delves into the lives of three generations of the Aquino family, tracing their impact on Philippine history and society. Joaquin explores the intertwined destinies of the Aquino family and the nation, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and enduring legacy. He offers readers a deeper understanding of the complexities of Philippine politics, society, and identity, while also paying homage to the courage and resilience of the Aquino family in the face of adversity.

Summary

Joaquin's narrative takes readers on a journey through the tumultuous events that shaped the Aquino family's fortunes, from the early 20th century to the present day. He examines the roles played by prominent figures such as Benigno Aquino Sr., a respected politician and patriarch of the family; his son, Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., a charismatic opposition leader who fought against the Marcos dictatorship; and Ninoy's wife, Corazon "Cory" Aquino, who became the first female president of the Philippines and a symbol of democracy.
