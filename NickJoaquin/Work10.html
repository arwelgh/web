Description

One of the most prominent Filipino writers of the 20th century. Published in 1961, it is considered one of Joaquin's most significant works and a masterpiece of Philippine literature. The novel is set in Manila during the early 20th century, a time of great social and political upheaval in the Philippines. It revolves around the character of Consuelo, a wealthy and enigmatic woman who believes she possesses two navels, a physical anomaly that reflects her inner turmoil and search for identity.

Summary

As Consuelo grapples with her dual identity and the secrets of her past, she encounters a cast of characters who represent various facets of Filipino society, from the wealthy elite to the marginalized poor. Their intertwining stories shed light on the interconnectedness of Philippine history and the enduring impact of colonialism on the Filipino psyche. It celebrated for its rich symbolism, intricate narrative structure, and profound exploration of Filipino identity. It has been praised for its lyrical prose and powerful storytelling, cementing Joaquin's reputation as one of the most important voices in Philippine literature.
